BJP / NDA 2014–2024 vs Congress / UPA 2004–2014

India Governance Report Card: 79 Categories

Comprehensive, source-backed, unbiased analysis of key government performance indicators

Sources: World Bank, NSSO/PLFS, RBI, MHA, NCRB, RSF, SATP, NFHS, DPIIT, CAG, PIB, CMIE, ADB, IMF

IMPORTANT CONTEXT:
UPA era (2004-2014) benefited from a global economic boom pre-2008 (world GDP growth ~5.6%). NDA era (2014-2024) was impacted by COVID-19 pandemic (-7.3% GDP in FY21) and lower global growth (~4%). Many NDA welfare achievements build directly on UPA foundations (MGNREGA, Aadhaar, RTI, NRHM). GDP back-series data is contested. Winner determinations use best available objective data and note where comparison is disputed.

Overall Verdict (Unbiased Assessment)

BJP/NDA Strengths (52 categories)

  • Infrastructure: Highways, airports, metro, railways
  • Inflation control: CPI 5.1% vs UPA's 8.1%
  • National security: 80% less terrorism deaths
  • Welfare delivery: Electricity, toilets, water, Ayushman
  • Economy size: 10th to 5th globally
  • FDI: $596B vs $306B
  • Digitisation: UPI, DBT, JAM Trinity
  • Renewable energy: 7x capacity growth
  • Foreign policy: G20, Quad, global influence
  • Ease of Business: 142 to 63 rank

Congress/UPA Strengths (7 categories)

  • Press freedom: Better RSF rankings (120 vs 157)
  • Internet shutdowns: Minimal vs world's highest under NDA
  • Freedom of expression: Fewer political prosecutions
  • Real wage growth: Better rural wages 2006-2012
  • MMR reduction: Faster rate under NRHM
  • Debt-to-GDP: Lower average government debt
  • Political funding: More transparent than Electoral Bonds

Important Caveats (20 ties)

  • GDP: UPA benefited from global boom; NDA hit by COVID
  • NDA welfare builds on UPA's MGNREGA, Aadhaar, NRHM
  • Unemployment data methodology changed; not directly comparable
  • Poverty data contested; World Bank estimates disputed
  • Learning outcomes poor under both governments
  • Air quality worsened under both; only measurement improved
  • Caste violence: Higher numbers may mean better reporting
  • Judicial independence concerns under both governments
Balanced Conclusion: On quantitative developmental indicators — infrastructure, inflation, poverty reduction, national security, and welfare delivery — the BJP/NDA government (2014-2024) outperforms the Congress/UPA government on the majority of the 79 metrics analysed. However, on democratic health indicators — press freedom, internet shutdowns, freedom of expression, and political funding transparency — the record is significantly worse under NDA, representing a concerning democratic backslide. Both governments had genuine achievements; the UPA foundations in MGNREGA, NRHM, Aadhaar, and RTI were indispensable to NDA's later welfare gains.
